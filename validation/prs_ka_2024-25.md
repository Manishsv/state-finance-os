## 3. Discrepancy Analysis

### 3.1 Total Receipts: 0.09% gap (₹250 cr)

PRS's "Total Receipts (excluding borrowings)" includes:
- All Revenue Receipts (own tax, central transfers, non-tax)
- **Plus non-debt Capital Receipts** (recovery of loans, miscellaneous capital
  receipts)

Our `Total Revenue (I+II)` figure is revenue receipts only. The ₹250 cr gap is
likely the recovery-of-loans component, which sits under Appendix-3 in RBI's
classification (capital receipts) but is consolidated by PRS into the "total
receipts" figure.

**Resolution:** [METHODOLOGY] — neither is wrong. We report a tighter, more
analytically useful number; PRS reports a broader headline.

**v1 action:** Add a derived metric `total_non_debt_receipts` that matches
PRS's headline. Already computable from existing data.

### 3.2 Total Expenditure: 0.86% gap (₹2,975 cr)

PRS's "Total Expenditure (excluding debt repayment)" includes:
- Revenue Expenditure (matches)
- Capital Outlay (matches)
- **Plus Loans and Advances given by State Government** (Appendix-4
  Section IV, ~₹3,000 cr for KA), which is "non-debt capital
  disbursement" but not "capital outlay" in the strict sense

Our `Total Capital Outlay` excludes loans-given. The ₹2,975 cr gap matches
the Loans-and-Advances head almost exactly.

**Resolution:** [METHODOLOGY] — terminology gap. "Total Capital Outlay"
(narrow, asset-creating) vs "Capital Disbursements ex Debt" (broader,
includes loans-given). Both are used in different parts of state budget
documents.

**v1 action:** Add a metric `total_expenditure_excl_debt` matching PRS.

### 3.3 Social Welfare & Nutrition: 2.93% gap (₹1,326 cr) — UNKNOWN

PRS reports ₹45,281 cr; our `I.A.9 (Social Sec & Welfare) + I.A.10
(Nutrition)` = ₹43,955 cr. The ₹1,326 cr gap could be:

1. **PRS includes capital outlay on housing / SC-ST welfare** — Appendix-4
   has heads `I.1.a.5: Housing` and `I.1.a.7: Welfare of SCs/STs/OBCs` in
   capital outlay. Need to check if their sum approximates ₹1,326 cr.
2. **PRS includes Welfare of Scheduled Castes (I.A.7) partially** — that
   head alone is ₹10,123 cr, so it can't be fully included; possibly only
   the Schedule-Caste sub-component.
3. **PRS uses a Karnataka-specific "Social Welfare & Nutrition" demand-for-
   grants line** — Karnataka's budget has its own demand groupings that
   may not align with RBI's I.A.9 / I.A.10 head split.

**Action:** investigate by reading the PRS PDF's full Annex (sectoral table
§3 of the brief) to see which sub-heads they aggregate. Likely a
**[METHODOLOGY]** discrepancy from KA-specific demand grouping, but worth
confirming.

### 3.4 GSDP: 2.66% gap (₹74,840 cr)

PRS quotes ₹28,09,063 cr — this is the **state's own GSDP projection** from
the Karnataka Budget Speech 2024-25. We use ₹28,83,903 cr — RBI Handbook
Table 21 (Current Prices) compiled from NSO data.

The two numbers measure conceptually identical things but differ in:
- **Vintage:** State's projection was made in early 2024 (during budget
  prep); RBI's compilation is dated December 2025 and reflects subsequent
  NSO revisions.
- **Methodology:** State Statistics Departments and NSO use slightly
  different industry-classification handling for some sub-sectors.

**Resolution:** [METHODOLOGY]. Neither is wrong. The state's number is
what was used to make budget decisions; RBI's number is the ex-post
authoritative figure. For *fiscal-effort* metrics like Tax/GSDP, NSO/RBI
is the standard denominator (Finance Commission methodology).

**v1 action:** None needed. We already cite the source explicitly via
`source_id = rbi.handbook.2024-25`.

## 4. Gap Analysis: PRS Coverage We Don't Have

PRS's brief addresses these dimensions; FinanceOS does not (yet):

| PRS dimension | Why we're missing it | Effort to add |
|---|---|---|
| **Fiscal Deficit** (₹82,981 cr / 3% of GSDP) | Requires Revenue Deficit + Net Capital Disbursements + Loans Given. All inputs in store; just need a metric. | 30 min |
| **Outstanding Liabilities (debt stock as % of GSDP)** | Need debt stock from Annual Financial Statement, not in RBI e-STATES Appendix-3 (which is only flow data). | New driver — half day |

| **Specific scheme allocations** (PM-Awas, MGNREGA, Anna Bhagya) | Schemes cut across Major Heads; need a separate `schemes` registry. | Multi-day |
| **Political-economy commentary** (election context, policy promises) | Out of scope by design. | n/a |
| **Budget speech promise tracking** (what was announced vs what was budgeted) | Out of scope by design. | n/a |

## 5. Coverage We Have That PRS Doesn't

PRS's brief is one PDF per state, ~10 pages, mostly KA-internal. FinanceOS
exposes dimensions PRS's static brief format cannot:

| Our dimension | What it adds beyond PRS |
|---|---|
| **Cross-state peer ranking** on 54 metrics | PRS shows comparison tables for ~10 indicators; we surface 54 with quartile flags |
| **Tax Mix decomposition** (SGST / Excise / Stamps / VAT / Vehicles share) | PRS reports own-tax total only |
| **Borrowing Source Mix** (Market Loans / NSSF / Centre / WMA / NABARD) | PRS reports gross borrowing; we decompose by source |
| **Capital Outlay Sectoral Split** (% of capex per sector) | PRS reports absolute sector allocations; we report capex-share |
| **Multi-year CAGR trends** (5-year, ACT) | PRS does YoY only |
| **Macro & Per-Capita** (Tax/GSDP, Capex/Capita, etc.) | PRS reports some macro ratios; we automate all |
| **Net Power Subsidy proxy** | PRS does not report this |
| **Revenue Load (vs Own Tax Revenue)** family | PRS does not report (% of own-tax view of various costs) |

## 7. Validation Methodology

This validation:

1. Fetched PRS's published headline numbers from the brief's web page and
   PDF (cached for reproducibility).
2. Queried our store for KA 2024-25 BE values via `budget_signals`.
3. Combined revenue + capital expenditure per sector to match PRS's
   "total allocation" presentation.
4. Did NOT modify any FinanceOS code or values to force matches. Where
   numbers diverge, we report the divergence.

What this validation does *not* check:
- ACT comparisons (PRS publishes BE-time briefs; ACT comparison would
  require their later publications)
- Sub-component drill-down within sectors (PRS may break Education into
  School / Higher Ed / Technical Ed; we have not yet tested that level)
- Multi-year consistency (PRS publishes one year per brief; trend
  validation needs cross-edition reading)
